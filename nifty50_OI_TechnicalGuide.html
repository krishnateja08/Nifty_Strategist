Nifty 50 OI & Technical Dashboard

A complete guide to reading every tab, chart, signal, and number in your dashboard.

00 — What This Dashboard Does

This script fetches live data from NSE (option chain), yfinance (price & technicals), and Groww/NSE (FII/DII flows), then generates a single index.html file that serves as your complete intraday trading dashboard for Nifty 50.

Every time you run the script, it takes a fresh snapshot. The HTML page auto-refreshes when it detects a new run (via latest_report.json timestamp polling every 30 seconds).

What you get in one glance

A directional bias (BUY / SELL / NEUTRAL), confidence level, stop-loss, two targets, strategy suggestions, key support/resistance, PCR, RSI, MACD, SMA alignment, FII/DII flows, India VIX, heatmap of all 50 stocks, and a full intraday OI history with sparkline charts.

04 — 📊 OI Analysis

Three cards showing raw OI change data from the current NSE option chain snapshot:

The Three OI Cards

CE OI Change (Total Call OI Δ): Sum of OI changes across all call strikes. Positive = new call writing (bearish — writers expect price won't go above). Negative = call unwinding (bullish).

PE OI Change (Total Put OI Δ): Sum of OI changes across all put strikes. Positive = new put writing (bullish — writers expect price won't fall below). Negative = put unwinding (bearish).

Net OI Change (CE Δ − PE Δ): Positive = more call writing than put writing = bearish. Negative = more put writing = bullish.

⚠️ OI Direction arrow: The coloured arrow and label ("Net Call Accumulation" = bearish, "Net Put Accumulation" = bullish) is derived from this net OI value and correctly interpreted in the card.

11 — 📊 Intraday OI Trend (Detailed Guide)

This is the most data-rich tab. Every time your script runs, it logs a snapshot to oi_log.json. This tab reads that file and builds a time-series view of how OI, signals, and price evolved throughout the day.

The Two Views: Focus vs Detail

Focus View (Default)

Shows only the essential columns: Time, Signal, PCR, Spot, Nifty Move %, and the Net OI Diff. Use this for quick scanning during trading hours.

Detail View

Adds extra columns: Call OI Δ, Put OI Δ, Diff (numeric), VWAP, Futures Price. Use this when you want to understand why a signal changed.

💡 Your view choice now persists across auto-refreshes (saved in URL hash). Click Detail once and it stays on Detail until you switch back.

How to Use This Tab for Trading

Practical workflow

1. Check the sparkline first. Is the fill predominantly green or red? That's your intraday OI bias.

2. Look at signal consistency. If the signal has been BUY for the last 5+ readings, OI conviction is strong. If it's flipping every reading, stay out.

3. Check PCR trend. PCR steadily rising from 0.8 → 1.2 = put writers gaining confidence = bullish build-up.

4. Confirm with spot movement. If signals say BUY but spot is falling — either it's early accumulation (wait) or a false signal (be cautious).

5. Switch to 15-min or 1-hr to see the bigger picture if 3-min data is noisy.

12 — 📅 Weekly Outlook (Detailed Guide)

This tab projects where Nifty might head over the coming week by combining 7 independent methods and clustering their outputs into high-conviction zones. It does not predict direction — it maps out where price is likely to stall in each scenario.

Weekly Scenario Projections

Three cards show what to expect in each scenario:

📈 Bullish Scenario

Target 1: The nearest resistance cluster above the current price with the most confluences. This is where an upward move is most likely to pause first.

Target 2: The next resistance cluster further above. Price reaching here requires strong momentum.

Each target shows a strength badge (STRONG/MODERATE/WEAK) and confluence tags listing which methods agree at that level (e.g., "Pivot R1", "CE Wall", "Fib 38.2%").

📉 Bearish Scenario

Target 1: The nearest support cluster below current price. If the market falls, it's likely to find a floor here first.

Target 2: Deeper support. A break below Target 1 could send price to this level.

↔️ Sideways Range

If the market has no clear direction, it's expected to oscillate within this range. Derived from ATR and VIX implied weekly move. Useful for non-directional strategies like Iron Condors and Short Strangles.

Confluence Map (Visual Bar)

A horizontal bar showing all clustered levels as dots positioned relative to the current price:

● Green dotsSupport levels (below price)
● Red dotsResistance levels (above price)

Larger, brighter dots = more confluences at that zone. The dashed box shows the expected sideways range.

💡 How to use: If price is approaching a large bright dot, expect resistance/support there. If there are no large dots nearby in one direction, the move could be explosive.

Weekly Pivot Points

Calculated from the previous week's High, Low, and Close. Three types are available via toggle buttons:

Pivot Types

Classic Pivots: The most widely used by institutional traders. PP is the balance point; R1/R2/R3 are resistance levels; S1/S2/S3 are supports. Best for swing and positional trades.

Fibonacci Pivots: Uses the same PP but applies Fibonacci ratios (0.382, 0.618, 1.0) to the previous week's range. Best when you expect price to respect golden-ratio retracement zones.

Camarilla Pivots: Tighter levels, best for intraday. R3/S3 are reversal points (price likely bounces), R4/S4 are breakout points (price likely accelerates through).

Each row shows the level, its price, and the distance from current spot in both points and percentage.

Level Confluence Clusters

This is the most important table. It takes all levels from all 7 methods, groups ones within 50 points of each other, and shows them as a single zone.

How Clustering Works

The script gathers levels from: Classic Pivots, Fibonacci Pivots, Camarilla Pivots, Fibonacci Retracement, OI Walls (CE/PE max OI, support/resistance), SMA 20/50/200, ATR bounds, and VIX bounds.

Any levels within 50 points of each other are grouped into one cluster. The cluster's strength depends on how many independent methods contributed:

STRONG3+ methods agree — highest conviction zone. Price is very likely to react here.
MODERATE2 methods agree — decent zone, but may not hold under heavy momentum.
WEAKSingle indicator only — treat as a minor level, not a trading decision on its own.

How to Use This Tab for Trading

Practical Workflow

1. Check the current bias from the Signals tab. If it's BUY → focus on bullish targets. SELL → focus on bearish targets. SIDEWAYS → use the neutral range.

2. Set your targets from the scenario card. Use Target 1 for conservative trades, Target 2 for aggressive.

3. Check confluence strength. Only trade towards STRONG zones (3+ confluences). WEAK targets are unreliable.

4. Use the Confluence Map to visually see where the "walls" are. If you're buying and there's a large red dot just 50 points above, the risk-reward may be poor.

5. Cross-reference with intraday data. The weekly outlook gives you the map; the Intraday OI tab tells you where you are on that map right now.

⚠️ Important: These are probable zones, not predictions. The market can and will break through any level. Always use stop-losses and confirm with live data before entering trades.

15 — Signal Engine Logic

The OI signal (shown in the Intraday OI table) is generated through a 4-step decision tree:

Step 1 — Opposite Directions (Clearest Signal)
CE ΔPE ΔSignalWhy
+ve (building)−ve (unwinding)STRONG SELLNew calls + puts leaving = true bearish
−ve (unwinding)+ve (building)STRONG BUYCalls leaving + new puts = true bullish
Step 2 — Both Building (Check Dominance)

If both CE Δ and PE Δ are positive, whichever side is 1.5× larger dominates:

PE Δ > CE Δ × 1.5 → BUY  |  CE Δ > PE Δ × 1.5 → SELL

If neither dominates → falls through to Step 3 (PCR + VWAP + Trend tiebreaker).

Step 3 — PCR + VWAP + PCR Trend Tiebreaker

When neither side dominates, the engine uses PCR thresholds combined with whether spot is above/below VWAP, and a 5-reading PCR trend analysis:

PCR > 1.5STRONG BUY (if above VWAP) or BUY
PCR 1.1 – 1.5BUY if above VWAP, else checks VWAP gap
PCR 0.9 – 1.1Uses PCR trend (rising/falling over last 5 readings) to break tie
PCR 0.5 – 0.9SELL if below VWAP, else checks VWAP gap
PCR < 0.5STRONG SELL (if below VWAP) or SELL
Step 4 — Both Unwinding

If both CE Δ and PE Δ are negative (both sides unwinding), there's no conviction either way → NEUTRAL

16 — PCR (Put-Call Ratio) — How to Read

PCR = Total Put OI ÷ Total Call OI

PCR RangeInterpretation
> 1.5Extreme put writing. Institutions building a strong floor. Very bullish.
1.1 – 1.5More puts than calls. Bullish bias.
0.9 – 1.1Balanced. No clear edge. Use other indicators.
0.5 – 0.9More calls than puts. Bearish bias.
< 0.5Extreme call writing. Institutions building a ceiling. Very bearish.
💡 Common mistake: High PCR does NOT mean "too many puts = bearish." Option writers (sellers) are the smart money. They write puts when they believe price will stay above. High put writing = bullish floor.

17 — Sparkline Chart — Complete Guide

The sparkline plots PE Δ − CE Δ over time as a filled area chart:

Reading the Chart

Green area above zero: Put writing exceeds call writing. Market is building a bullish floor. The taller the green, the stronger the conviction.

Red area below zero: Call writing exceeds put writing. Market is building a bearish ceiling. The deeper the red, the stronger the conviction.

Crossing the zero line: Sentiment is flipping. Pay close attention — this is where trend changes begin.

The yellow/orange line: Traces the actual diff value. When it's trending consistently in one direction, momentum is building.

The green price line: Nifty spot overlay. Compare direction with the fill — convergence = confirmation, divergence = potential reversal.

🔴 Divergence alert: If the sparkline is deep green (bullish OI) but the price line is falling — institutions may be wrong OR accumulation is happening before a bounce. Wait for price confirmation before acting.

18 — Momentum Override

Sometimes OI signals lag during sharp V-shaped reversals. The momentum override catches this:

How It Works

The engine checks the last 4–6 spot price readings. If price has moved consistently in one direction for 4+ consecutive readings while the OI signal says the opposite:

Hard override (>0.5% move)Signal flips entirely (SELL → BUY or vice versa)
Soft override (0.25–0.5%)Signal becomes NEUTRAL (removes false conviction)

This prevents you from holding a stale SELL signal while the market is rallying 100+ points.
