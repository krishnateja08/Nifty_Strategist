MODULE 01 // OPTIONS STRATEGIES

Options Strategies Guide

Learn how to use options not just as a bet, but as a precision weapon. From simple long calls to advanced iron condors — every strategy explained with real Nifty trade examples.

📖 What Are Options?

An option is a contract that gives you the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset (like Nifty 50) at a specific price (called the Strike Price) before or on a specific date (Expiry).

Think of it like this: You see a flat for ₹50 lakh. You pay ₹50,000 to the owner to "lock the price" for 30 days. If prices rise to ₹60 lakh, you exercise your right and buy at ₹50L. If prices fall, you just lose the ₹50,000 — your premium. That's exactly how a Call Option works.

📞 CALL OPTION

Right to BUY at strike price. You buy a Call when you expect the market to go UP. Your max loss = Premium paid. Profit = Unlimited upside.

📤 PUT OPTION

Right to SELL at strike price. You buy a Put when you expect the market to go DOWN. Your max loss = Premium paid. Profit = Unlimited downside.

🦋 Butterfly Spread

A Butterfly profits when the market expires exactly at your chosen strike. Buy 1 ITM Call, Sell 2 ATM Calls, Buy 1 OTM Call. Very low cost, very precise.

📅 Calendar Spread

Sell a near-week option and buy a far-week option at the same strike. Profits from time decay. The near-term option decays faster, and you pocket the difference.

💀 The 5 Ways Traders Blow Their Account

1

Over-leveraging

Taking a ₹2L position with ₹10,000 capital. One bad trade = total wipeout. Futures margin gives 10x leverage — most beginners don't respect this.

2

No Stop Loss

"It will come back" — the most expensive 5 words in trading. Refusing to accept a small loss leads to catastrophic losses.

3

Overtrading

Taking 10-15 trades a day trying to "recover" losses. Each trade has brokerage + slippage. The house always wins with overtraders.

4

Averaging Down Losers

Buying more as the stock falls. "Sahi ho jayega." This turns small losses into massive ones. Never average a losing trade without a plan.

5

No Daily/Weekly Loss Limit

No cap on how much you'll lose in a day. A bad day can erase a week of gains. Professionals set hard daily loss limits.

MODULE 02 // POSITION SIZING

Position Sizing

How much to put into each trade. This single rule separates professionals from gamblers. Get this right, and no single trade can ruin you.

📐 The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your total trading capital on any single trade. This means even if you have 10 losing trades in a row, you still have 90% of your capital intact.

Position Size = (Account Size × Risk %) ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Loss Price) EXAMPLE: ₹1,00,000 ACCOUNT × 1% = ₹1,000 MAX RISK PER TRADE

POSITION SIZING — Real Calculation

🧮 Kelly Criterion (Advanced)

The Kelly formula tells you the optimal % of capital to risk based on your win rate and average win/loss ratio.

Kelly % = W − [(1 − W) ÷ R] W = WIN RATE | R = AVERAGE WIN ÷ AVERAGE LOSS

Kelly Calculation Example

Your stats: Win rate = 55% (W=0.55), Avg win = ₹3,000, Avg loss = ₹1,500 (R=2)

Kelly = 0.55 − [(1−0.55) ÷ 2] = 0.55 − 0.225 = 0.325 = 32.5%

Kelly says risk 32.5% per trade — that's too aggressive. Most pros use Half Kelly (16%) or even quarter Kelly for safety.

MODULE 02 // STOP LOSS MASTERY

Stop Loss Mastery

A stop loss is your airbag. You hope you never need it, but the day you do — it saves you. Placing it correctly is an art.

🚨 Types of Stop Losses

📍 FIXED STOP LOSS

Set at a fixed ₹ amount or % from entry. Simple but doesn't account for market structure. E.g.: Always exit if trade goes ₹2,000 against you.

📊 TECHNICAL STOP LOSS

Placed below key support/above key resistance. Respects market structure. E.g.: Nifty entry at 24,000, stop below 23,780 support level.

⏰ TIME-BASED STOP LOSS

Exit if trade doesn't work within expected time. E.g.: Bought Nifty call for a rally — if no move in 2 days, exit regardless of P&L.

🔄 TRAILING STOP LOSS

Move stop up as trade profits. Locks in gains while letting winners run. E.g.: Buy at 24,000, target 24,400. Shift stop to 23,900 once 24,100 reached.

TECHNICAL STOP LOSS — Nifty Trade Example

Nifty breaks out above 24,000 with strong volume. Support zone = 23,780–23,820.

🧠 Stop Loss Psychology

  • ✓Set stop loss BEFORE entering — never decide after. Once in a trade, emotions cloud judgment.
  • ✓Never move stop loss wider because you "feel" it will come back. If your stop is wrong, exit and re-analyze.
  • ✓Respect your stop even if it triggers and the market reverses. The market doesn't owe you anything.
  • ✗Never trade without a stop loss in futures/options — one bad gap-down open can destroy weeks of work.
  • !Trailing stops are powerful but don't trail too tight — you'll get stopped out of great moves on noise.
MODULE 02 // RISK:REWARD RATIO

⚡ Understanding R:R Ratio

R:R ratio compares how much you risk to how much you stand to gain. A 1:2 R:R means for every ₹1,000 you risk, you target ₹2,000 profit.

R:R Ratio = Target Profit ÷ Stop Loss Amount TARGET = ₹5,000 | STOP LOSS = ₹2,500 | R:R = 1:2 ✅

MODULE 02 // DRAWDOWN & RECOVERY

Drawdown & Recovery

Drawdown is the peak-to-trough decline in your account. Managing it correctly separates traders who survive long-term from those who blow up.

📉 The Brutal Math of Recovery

Most traders don't realize how hard it is to recover from large losses. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain just to get back to break even!

🚨 HARD RULE: MAX DRAWDOWN LIMIT = 20%

If your account drops 20% from its peak, STOP TRADING. Take a break, review your trades, find what went wrong. Never try to "trade your way out" of a deep drawdown — it leads to desperate decisions and further losses.

Managing a Losing Streak — Real Example

Account: ₹1,00,000. You've had 5 losing trades in a row. Down ₹8,000 (8%).

1

Reduce position size by 50%

If you were risking ₹1,000/trade, drop to ₹500. Give yourself room to breathe and rebuild confidence.

2

Review your last 5 trades in journal

Is the market in a different regime? Was there a pattern in your losses? Entry too early? Stop too tight?

3

Sit out 1–2 days

Sometimes the market just isn't suitable for your strategy. No trade is a trade. Cash is a position.

4

Re-enter only when setup is A+ quality

Don't trade to "get even." Trade only when all your checklist boxes are ticked. Be selective.

🧠 The Emotional Cycle of a Trader

Every new trader goes through the same emotional journey. Recognizing where you are in this cycle is the first step to breaking free from it.

1

Optimism & Excitement

New trader. "I've found the perfect strategy." Makes a few wins. Feels invincible. Increases size too fast.

2

Anxiety & Denial

Losses start. "It's just a bad patch." Holds losers longer. Hopes. Averages down. Capital erodes slowly.

3

Panic & Desperation

Big loss. Revenge trades. Abandons strategy. Takes random trades trying to recover. Account bleeds rapidly.

4

Acceptance & Learning

Hits rock bottom. Studies more. Starts journaling. Reduces size. Accepts losses as cost of doing business.

5

Mastery & Consistency

Process over outcome. Follows rules. Small, consistent gains. No ego. Trades like a machine, not a gambler.

💡 KEY INSIGHT

The best traders are not the ones who are never afraid or greedy. They're the ones who recognize the emotion, and trade their plan anyway. Emotion is information — not instruction.

MODULE 03 // FOMO & GREED

FOMO & Greed

Fear Of Missing Out and Greed are the two deadliest emotions in trading. They make you chase trades, hold winners too long, and ignore your plan.

🔥 FOMO — Fear of Missing Out

😱

The FOMO Trade

Nifty suddenly rallies 200 points. You're not in. You see the candle going up. Urgency takes over — "I'm missing this move!" You jump in near the top, just as it reverses.

FIX: Accept that you missed it. There will be ANOTHER setup. "I don't chase trades — I wait for trades to come to me." Write this on a sticky note next to your screen.

FOMO Trade — Real Cost

Nifty rallies from 23,800 to 24,200 in 30 minutes on budget news. You missed the initial entry.

The patient trader who waited for the breakout at 23,830 made ₹18,000. The FOMO trader lost ₹12,000 entering the same "rally." Timing from emotion = destruction.

💰 Greed — Moving Targets & Over-Leveraging

🤑

The Greed Trap

Your trade hits Target 1 (₹5,000 profit). Instead of booking, you think "It's going to 24,500 for sure!" You hold. Market reverses. You end up with ₹1,000 profit — or worse, a loss.

FIX: Book partial profits at Target 1 (50-60%). Move stop to breakeven. Let rest ride to Target 2. Never let a winner turn into a loser by waiting for "just a bit more."
📈

Over-Leveraging After Wins

You made ₹20,000 in a week. You feel invincible. You triple your position size. One loss wipes out the entire week's gains — plus more.

FIX: Increase position size gradually — max 10% increase after 3 consecutive profitable weeks. Never double size after one good trade. Respect the process.
MODULE 03 // FEAR & HESITATION

Fear & Hesitation

The other side of greed. Fear makes you miss good setups, exit winners too early, and freeze when you should act.

😰

Fear of Pulling the Trigger

Perfect setup forms. All indicators aligned. But after 3 losses last week, you hesitate. You watch the trade move without you. You enter late, after confirmation — just as it reverses.

FIX: Trust your system. If your edge has proven itself over 50+ trades, take the next trade without hesitation. A small position size helps — you can't be paralyzed by a trade you barely risk on.
🏃

Exiting Winners Too Early

You're up ₹4,000 in a trade. Profit looks great. Fear of losing it kicks in. You exit at ₹4,000 but the trade runs to ₹15,000. You "got out" but missed the real move.

FIX: Let the chart decide your exit, not your emotions. If stop hasn't been hit, stay in. Trust the structure. Lock in gains with a trailing stop instead of a market exit out of fear.
🔄

Analysis Paralysis

You look at 12 indicators. They all say different things. Stochastic says buy, MACD says sell, RSI is neutral. You can't decide and do nothing — or worse, take a random trade out of frustration.

FIX: Simplify. Pick your top 3 confluences (e.g., trend + S/R level + one momentum indicator). Only trade when all 3 agree. Complexity is the enemy of execution.
MODULE 03 // REVENGE TRADING

Revenge Trading

The single most account-destroying behaviour in trading. It starts with one loss — and ends with many.

💢 What is Revenge Trading?

After a loss, an emotional impulse to "get the money back immediately" drives you to take another trade — usually bigger, more aggressive, without a proper setup. It's trading out of anger, not analysis.

The Revenge Spiral — How ₹5,000 Loss Becomes ₹40,000 Loss

1

9:30 AM — Trade 1

Normal setup. Buy Nifty 24,000 CE. Stop hits. Loss: −₹5,000. Running total: −₹5,000

2

9:45 AM — Trade 2 (Revenge)

No setup. Doubles size to recover. Angry, no patience. Stop hits again. Loss: −₹10,000. Total: −₹15,000

3

10:05 AM — Trade 3 (Desperate)

Triples size now. No stop loss set — "can't afford to lose again." Market gaps down. Loss: −₹18,000. Total: −₹33,000

4

10:30 AM — Trade 4 (Final)

All-in. No logic. Panic. Loss: −₹7,000. Final total: −₹40,000 in 1 hour

One disciplined exit at −₹5,000 could have ended the day. Instead, the entire account lost 40% in 60 minutes.

🛑 How to Stop Revenge Trading

  • ✓Daily loss limit = 2× average trade risk. Hit it? Platform is closed for the day. Non-negotiable.
  • ✓10-minute rule: After any loss, stand up, walk away for 10 minutes before even thinking about the next trade.
  • ✓Acknowledge the emotion: Say out loud — "I am angry right now. Angry traders lose. I will not trade angry."
  • ✓Shrink position size after a loss — not increase it. Smaller risk = clearer thinking.
  • ✗Never let yesterday's loss affect today's trade size or plan.
MODULE 03 // DISCIPLINE

Building Trading Discipline

Discipline is not willpower. It's a system. The best traders don't rely on motivation — they build habits and routines that make good decisions automatic.

📋 The Pre-Market Routine

1

8:30 AM — Market Prep (30 mins)

Check SGX Nifty, global cues (Dow, Nasdaq, crude). Read 2-3 key news items. No Twitter/social media.

2

9:00 AM — Chart Analysis

Mark key support/resistance levels on Nifty 15m, 1h, daily. Identify the daily bias (bullish/bearish/neutral).

3

9:10 AM — Trading Plan

Write down: "If Nifty does X, I will do Y with Z stop loss and A target." Plan the trade before it happens.

4

9:15 AM — Mindset Check

Are you tired? Emotional? Distracted? If yes — trade half size or don't trade. Your state of mind IS your edge.

5

9:15–9:30 AM — WAIT

Never trade the first 15 minutes. Let the market settle. Gaps fill, fake moves fade. Let professionals shake out the weak hands.

📜 The Trader's Rules — Print and Paste

  • ✓I will never risk more than 1-2% of my capital on any single trade.
  • ✓I will set my stop loss before entering every trade — no exceptions.
  • ✓I will stick to my daily loss limit. If hit, I close the platform and do something else.
  • ✓I will not trade the first 15 minutes of the market open.
  • ✓I will record every trade in my journal: entry, exit, reason, emotion.
  • ✗I will not revenge trade. A loss is a business expense. Move on.
  • ✗I will not increase position size to recover losses.
  • ✗I will not average down a losing trade without a pre-planned rule.
  • !I accept that losses are part of trading. My job is to manage risk, not avoid losses.
MODULE 03 // TRADE JOURNAL

The Trade Journal

Your journal is your personal trading coach. It shows you patterns in your behaviour — both good and bad — that no mentor can see. The best traders review their journal weekly.

📓 What to Record in Every Trade

📊 TRADE DATA

Date, Time, Instrument (Nifty/BankNifty), Strike, Entry Price, Exit Price, Stop Loss, Target, Lot Size, P&L, Brokerage.

🧠 EMOTION LOG

How were you feeling before the trade? During? After? Were you anxious? Confident? Did emotions affect any decision? Rate emotion 1-10.

📝 TRADE REASON

What was your setup? What signal triggered entry? Was it in line with the daily bias? Rate setup quality: A, B, or C grade.

🔍 POST-TRADE REVIEW

Did you follow your plan? What went right? What went wrong? What would you do differently? Max 3 bullet points per trade.
