GREEK 1 OF 4 — PRICE SENSITIVITY

Delta

📌 If Nifty moves ₹1 in any direction, Delta is exactly how much your option price moves.
🚗
Professional Analogy: Shadow of a Moving Car

Delta is like the shadow of Nifty. When Nifty (the car) moves, your option (the shadow) follows — but the shadow size depends on where you are. ATM option = shadow is exactly half the car. Deep ITM = shadow matches the car perfectly. Deep OTM = tiny shadow, barely moves no matter how fast the car goes.

🟢 OPTION BUYER — Delta Perspective
When Delta Works For You
You buy CE → Delta is +0.5 → Nifty rises → you profit ₹0.50 per ₹1 move × 65 lots. As Nifty keeps rising, Delta increases to 0.7, 0.9 → you profit faster and faster. That acceleration is Gamma working for you.
When Delta Hurts You
You buy CE → Nifty falls → Delta still 0.5 but now works against you. For every ₹1 Nifty falls, CE loses ₹0.50. AND as Nifty keeps falling, Delta shrinks toward 0 — saving you from total loss, but you still bleed daily from Theta.
Professional Rule for Buyers
Buy ATM or slightly ITM options (Delta 0.4–0.6). Don't buy deep OTM (Delta 0.10) thinking it's cheap — Nifty needs to move 500+ pts just to make these profitable. It's a lottery ticket, not a trade.
🟠 OPTION SELLER — Delta Perspective
When Delta Works For You
You sell PE → Delta = −0.5 (from your position) → Nifty rises → PE loses value → you profit. Bull Put Spread is designed around this: sell ATM PE, buy OTM PE. Delta favours you when Nifty stays flat or rises.
When Delta Hurts You
You sell PE → Nifty drops → Delta accelerates against you via Gamma. Every ₹1 Nifty falls costs you more than the previous ₹1. This compounding is why sellers need strict stop losses, not hope.
Professional Rule for Sellers
Short Straddle has Delta ≈ 0. You are not taking a directional view — you are selling pure time value. The moment Nifty moves 150+ pts, your Delta becomes non-zero and you have a directional exposure. Exit or hedge immediately.

Monday 9:15 AM — Nifty at 23,150 BUY 1 lot Nifty 23150 CE (ATM) Premium paid = ₹233 × 65 = ₹15,145 Delta = 0.50 ───── 11 AM: Nifty at 23,350 (+200pts) ───── Delta P&L = 0.50 × 200 × 65 = +₹6,500 profit But Delta grew too (Gamma effect): Delta now = 0.68 (moved from 0.5 → 0.68) Actual P&L ≈ +₹8,450 ← more than linear 55% profit on investment in 2 hours
📊 Real Example — Seller Benefits From Delta
Monday — Nifty at 23,150 SELL Bull Put Spread Sell 23150 PE, Buy 23100 PE Credit collected = ₹1,534 Net Delta = −0.03 (nearly neutral) ──── Wednesday: Nifty at 23,300 (+150pts) ──── Both PEs moved OTM → worthless Nifty didn't need to move much. Just needed to STAY ABOVE 23,150. Full profit = +₹1,534 ✅ Delta protected you — position was nearly flat, you won on TIME not direction
💡
Pro Trader Rule: Delta is your directional exposure meter. +Delta = net long (bullish). −Delta = net short (bearish). Zero Delta = market-neutral. Professional sellers always aim for near-zero net Delta — they make money on Theta and IV crush, not on predicting direction. If your Delta is large, you're not selling premium — you're speculating.

GREEK 2 OF 4 — TIME DECAY

Theta

📌 Every single day that passes, your option loses a fixed amount of value — even if Nifty doesn't move 1 point. Theta is that daily loss (or gain for sellers).
🧊
Professional Analogy: The Melting Ice Cube

Every option is a melting ice cube. From the moment you buy it, it starts shrinking — slowly at first, then rapidly in the final days. Buyers are holding that ice and watching it melt. Sellers already cashed out the ice water — they profit as the cube melts, and pocket everything left by expiry. The question is always: does the buyer's gain from Nifty moving outpace the daily melt?

High reward, high danger. Professional sweet spot: Enter Monday, exit Wednesday
🟢 OPTION BUYER — Theta Perspective
Theta is Your Biggest Silent Killer
You buy a CE for ₹233. Even if Nifty sits completely flat for 3 days, that CE is now worth ₹180. You lost ₹53 per unit = ₹3,445 without Nifty moving at all. This is the cost of holding the option — like paying daily rent on a position.
The Breakeven Speed Problem
If you buy ATM CE on Monday at ₹233, you need Nifty to move fast enough to offset ₹5,728/day of straddle decay. Nifty must move at least 80–100pts just to break even by end of day. If it moves slowly, Theta eats your profit.
When Buyers Beat Theta
When a big news event causes Nifty to move 300–400pts in a single session, buyers win decisively. Theta only collected 1 day of decay, but Delta × 300pts created massive profit. Big fast moves are the buyer's best friend.
🟠 OPTION SELLER — Theta Perspective
Theta is Your Passive Income Engine
You sell Short Straddle on Monday. Theta earns you +₹5,728 per day. Monday night you sleep — wake up ₹5,728 richer. No screen time. No analysis. Just time passing. 3 days × ₹5,728 = ₹17,184 earned if Nifty stays flat. This is why sellers love quiet markets.
The Gamma vs Theta Race
Near expiry, Theta accelerates — but so does Gamma. Monday: Theta wins comfortably. Wednesday afternoon: race is close. Thursday: Gamma starts winning. The professional exits when Gamma risk overtakes Theta reward — not when greed says "one more day."
Professional Seller Strategy
Enter Monday at 9:30 AM after gap settles. Target 35–40% of max profit (not 100%). Exit Wednesday 12 PM regardless. Never hold to expiry. Repeat every week. Monthly income, not monthly gamble. Consistency beats greed every single time.

You earned ₹17,184. 53% of max profit in 3 days. → This is the textbook seller's exit. ✅
💡
Pro Trader Rule: Never buy options with less than 5 days to expiry unless you expect a massive move TODAY. Theta is exponential in the final days — you are fighting physics. Sellers: the best theta harvest is Monday entry to Wednesday exit for weekly options. You capture 60–70% of the theta decay with only 40% of the gamma risk. That asymmetry is the entire edge.

GREEK 3 OF 4 — VOLATILITY SENSITIVITY

Vega

📌 If India VIX moves by 1 point, Vega tells you exactly how much your option changes in value — regardless of whether Nifty moved at all.
🏠
Professional Analogy: The Rent Market

Think of option premium as rent. When you SELL options, you are the landlord collecting rent. High VIX = expensive neighbourhood = high rent income. But if VIX spikes after you lock in your rent, new market rent is higher. Your tenants (buyers) now pay more than you collected. You have to buy them out at a loss to exit — even if the property (Nifty) didn't change. Sell when rent is high. Exit before rent rises further.

🟢 OPTION BUYER — Vega Perspective
VIX Spike = Buyer's Windfall
You buy CE when VIX = 14. Surprise news hits → VIX jumps to 24. Even if Nifty barely moved, your CE doubled in price because everyone is suddenly scared and willing to pay more for options. Vega gifted you a profit without a Nifty move.
Never Buy When VIX is Already High
If VIX = 26 (IVP 95%), options are already expensive. You are buying at peak rent. After the event passes, VIX crashes from 26 → 15. Your option loses 40% of value from IV crush alone — even if Nifty moved in your direction. This is the most common beginner mistake.
Best Buying Opportunity
Buy when VIX is at 52-week lows (IVP below 20%). Premium is cheap. Any event = VIX spikes = your option value explodes. You get both Delta gain AND Vega gain. Double benefit from the same move.
🟠 OPTION SELLER — Vega Perspective
IV Crush = Seller's Best Day
You sell before Budget day. VIX = 26, you collect ₹520/unit. Budget passes without drama. VIX crashes to 15 in 30 minutes. Your sold options are now worth ₹300/unit. You close for ₹14,300 profit — and Nifty only moved 80pts. IV crush did all the work.
VIX Spike After You Sell = Nightmare
You sell Straddle when VIX = 16. Unexpected bad news hits → VIX jumps to 25. Nifty only moved 50pts, but your options are now 60% more expensive. You must close at a big loss even though your directional view was right. Vega defeated you, not Delta.
The Professional Vega Play
Sell BEFORE big events (Budget, RBI, FOMC) when VIX is elevated. The event itself matters less than the VIX crash after it. Markets almost always see IV crush post-announcement regardless of outcome. Sell the fear, buy back the calm.

Sell options the day before the event (VIX is highest then). Buy them back the day after (IV crush completes within hours of announcement). This works regardless of which direction Nifty moves. Sell the uncertainty, buy back the certainty. That's the professional volatility trade.

GREEK 4 OF 4 — DELTA ACCELERATION

Gamma

📌 Gamma measures how fast your Delta changes when Nifty moves. It makes profits and losses NON-LINEAR — each move hurts (or helps) more than the one before it.
❄️
Professional Analogy: Snowball Rolling Downhill

Gamma is a snowball. At the top of the hill (Monday, 5 days to expiry), it's tiny — easy to stop, manageable risk. Every day it rolls, it collects more snow. By expiry morning (bottom of the hill), the snowball is enormous — a single 100pt Nifty move can wipe out your entire premium collected. Sellers must get off the hill before the snowball reaches them. That exit window is Wednesday noon for weekly options.

⏰ Driver 1: Time to Expiry
Same strike, same Nifty: 5 days left Gamma = 0.0008 3 days left Gamma = 0.0018 1 day left Gamma = 0.0052 Exp morning Gamma = 0.0180 22x bigger in 5 days Time is Gamma's fuel. Less time = more Gamma.
📍 Driver 2: Distance From Strike
Strike = 23,000. Nifty at: 23,000 (ATM) MAX Gamma 22,900 (−100) High Gamma 22,700 (−300) Medium 22,500 (−500) Low Gamma 22,000 (−1000)Very Low ATM = binary outcome. Pays or doesn't = high sensitivity.
📊 Driver 3: VIX Level
Higher VIX = LOWER Gamma Lower VIX = HIGHER Gamma VIX = 12 → Gamma HIGH VIX = 18 → Gamma MED VIX = 26 → Gamma LOW High VIX spreads risk wider. Low VIX = options knife-edge. Calm market = most Gamma danger.

🟢 OPTION BUYER — Gamma Perspective
Gamma is Your Accelerator
You buy CE. Nifty rises 100pts — you make ₹3,250. Nifty rises another 100pts — you make ₹4,500 (more than before, because Delta grew from 0.5 to 0.7 due to Gamma). Each 100pt move in your direction pays MORE than the last. Gamma is compounding profits for buyers.
Best Gamma Buying Scenario
Buy ATM CE or PE with 3–5 days to expiry just BEFORE a big expected move (Fed announcement, budget, earnings). Gamma is high enough to accelerate profits fast, and IV spike from the event adds Vega gains on top. Double rocket fuel.
Gamma Cuts Both Ways for Buyers
If Nifty moves against you, Gamma slows your losses (Delta shrinks toward zero). But combined with Theta eating you daily, even a wrong direction + high Gamma day still destroys buyers who hold too long. Always have a time stop: exit in 2–3 days max if move doesn't happen.
🟠 OPTION SELLER — Gamma Perspective
Gamma is Your Biggest Enemy
You sell Straddle. Nifty drops 100pts — you lose ₹3,200. Drops another 100pts — you lose ₹4,500. Another 100pts — ₹5,800. Each move costs MORE than the previous. You are short Gamma. Losses accelerate while Theta (your income) is linear. This asymmetry kills accounts.
The Expiry Day Trap
Straddle seller on expiry day thinking "just 3 hours left, almost free money." Then Nifty drops 200pts at 1 PM. Gamma is nuclear — you lose ₹18,000+ in 2 hours. The entire week's Theta income wiped. This scenario happens almost every week to overconfident sellers.
How Professionals Handle Gamma
Set a hard Delta limit. If net Delta exceeds ±0.15 from a move, exit immediately — no exceptions. The move already happened; you cannot get it back. A small loss today beats a catastrophic loss tomorrow. Capital preservation is the entire game for premium sellers.

⚡ Professional Gamma Rules for Short Straddle Sellers
1
Enter on Monday after 9:30 AM — wait for the gap and initial volatility to settle before selling. Never sell into a fast-moving open.
2
Hard exit rule: Wednesday 12 PM — regardless of P&L. Even if you're still at breakeven. Gamma accelerates violently from Wednesday afternoon. You cannot recover in the final 6 hours what Gamma can take in 30 minutes.
3
Stop loss: Nifty ±100pts from your strike — not ±200, not ±150. At 100pts, Delta has shifted and your position is no longer market-neutral. Exit and re-assess. The move may continue. Gamma will compound the loss on every further point.
4
Target: 35–40% of max profit — not 80%, not 100%. Taking half the theoretical profit with half the time reduces your Gamma exposure by 60–70%. That's professional risk management, not settling for less.
5
Never add lots after a loss — the classic "average down" trap for sellers. If Nifty moved 150pts against you, Gamma is now working against you exponentially. Adding more is not averaging — it's compounding a losing trade.
