🕯️ Price Action
The foundation of all technical analysis — reading the raw language of the market

Before you use any indicator, you must master price action. Price action is simply studying how price moves over time using candlestick charts. Every candlestick tells you a battle between buyers (bulls) and sellers (bears). After 1 year of trading, you should start focusing heavily here — it's where professional discretionary traders live.

💡 Pro Tip Indicators are derived FROM price — they lag behind. If you can read price action directly, you get signals earlier and cleaner. Spend at least 3 months focusing only on raw candlestick charts before adding indicators back.

Trends, Support & Resistance

✅ Uptrend Rules

  • Price makes Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL)
  • Buy dips to support — don't chase breakouts
  • The trend is your friend until it ends
  • 50 EMA should be below price in a healthy uptrend
  • Volume should increase on up moves, decrease on pullbacks

📉 Downtrend Rules

  • Price makes Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL)
  • Sell rallies to resistance — don't short breakdowns blindly
  • Avoid trying to "catch the falling knife"
  • 50 EMA should be above price in a downtrend
  • Volume should surge on down days — confirms selling
⚠️ Common Mistake New traders try to pick tops and bottoms. Don't. Wait for CONFIRMATION of a trend change (a higher low in a downtrend, or a lower high in an uptrend) before switching bias. This alone will save you from many losing trades.
📊 Chart Patterns
Multi-candle formations that signal continuation or reversal of trends

Chart patterns are formations made by price over days, weeks, or months. They show the psychology of market participants — accumulation, distribution, indecision, and conviction. After recognizing these on a chart, you combine them with volume and indicators to confirm trades.

🔶

Head & Shoulders

Most reliable reversal pattern. Three peaks — middle is highest (head). Neckline break = confirmation. Target = distance from head to neckline, projected downward.

Reversal
🔺

Ascending Triangle

Flat top resistance + rising lows. Buyers are getting more aggressive. Breakout above flat top = strong bullish signal. Volume should surge on breakout.

Continuation
🔻

Descending Triangle

Flat bottom support + lower highs. Sellers dominating. Breakdown below flat support = bearish continuation. High failure rate in bull markets — context matters.

Continuation
🚩

Bull Flag

Sharp move up (pole) → consolidation in a slight downward channel (flag) → breakout. One of the most reliable intraday patterns. The flag should have LOW volume.

Continuation
⬆️

Cup & Handle

Rounding bottom (cup) followed by a small pullback (handle) then breakout. Classic long-term pattern on daily/weekly charts. Popularized by William O'Neil.

Reversal
💎

Diamond Top

Rare but powerful reversal. Looks like a diamond shape at market tops. Combination of expanding then contracting volatility. Very reliable when identified correctly.

Reversal
📐

Wedge Patterns

Rising wedge = bearish (price rising but losing momentum). Falling wedge = bullish (price falling but selling exhausted). Both indicate compression before a breakout.

Both
↔️

Rectangle / Channel

Price bouncing between horizontal support and resistance. Trade the range OR wait for breakout. Breakouts from long rectangles are very powerful — big moves follow.

Both

📡 Technical Indicators
Tools to quantify momentum, trend, and mean reversion — use them to confirm, not predict

Indicators are mathematical calculations applied to price and volume data. They are NOT magic — they are tools. The key insight is that indicators confirm what price is already showing. Never trade an indicator signal that contradicts the price action trend.

⚠️ Indicator Overload Warning Using 5+ indicators is called "analysis paralysis." Professional traders typically use 2–3 complementary indicators maximum. Pick one trend indicator (e.g., Moving Average) + one momentum indicator (e.g., RSI) + one volume indicator (e.g., OBV). That's enough.

Moving Averages (MA)

The single most important indicator to master. Moving averages smooth out price data to show the trend direction.

Bollinger Bands

Three lines: a 20-period SMA (middle) with upper and lower bands 2 standard deviations away. They show volatility and potential extremes.

📏 Key Signals

  • Bands squeezing together = volatility contraction → big move coming
  • Price touching upper band + reversal candle = potential short setup
  • Price touching lower band + hammer = potential long setup
  • "Riding the band" — strong trends hug the outer band for weeks

📌 Pro Settings

  • Standard: 20 SMA, 2.0 standard deviation — works for most markets
  • Crypto traders use 20 SMA, 2.5 std dev due to higher volatility
  • Combine with RSI: BB touch + RSI extreme = high probability reversal
  • Combine squeeze with MACD for breakout trades
📦 Volume Analysis
Volume is the fuel behind price moves — without it, breakouts fail

Volume is the number of shares/contracts traded in a period. It's the most important confirmation tool in technical analysis. Price moves on high volume are meaningful. Price moves on low volume are suspect. This is the single most underutilized edge for retail traders.

💡 The Golden Rule Volume precedes price. Watch for unusual volume BEFORE a big move — it tells you smart money is positioning. If price breaks out without volume, treat it as a false breakout until proven otherwise.

OBV — On Balance Volume

OBV adds volume on up days and subtracts volume on down days. It's a running total that shows whether money is flowing in or out of an asset.

If Price Closes UP → OBV = Previous OBV + Current Volume
If Price Closes DOWN → OBV = Previous OBV − Current Volume

🔑 OBV rising while price flat = accumulation → breakout likely coming
🔑 OBV falling while price flat = distribution → breakdown likely coming

VWAP — Volume Weighted Average Price

The single most important intraday indicator. Every institutional desk watches VWAP. It's the average price weighted by volume for the current session.

Above VWAP = Bullish

  • Institutions are in profit — likely to hold or add
  • Pullbacks to VWAP are buy opportunities in uptrends
  • First test of VWAP from above often holds as support
  • Day traders prefer long trades when price is above VWAP

Below VWAP = Bearish

  • Institutions underwater — they may sell rallies to reduce loss
  • Bounces to VWAP are short opportunities in downtrends
  • First test of VWAP from below often gets rejected
  • Day traders prefer short trades when price is below VWAP
🛡️ Risk Management
The only topic that separates traders who survive from those who blow up — non-negotiable

You can be right only 40% of the time and still be profitable if your winners are bigger than your losers. Risk management is the engine that keeps you in the game long enough to let your edge play out. After 1 year of trading, this should be your OBSESSION.

⚠️ Reality Check 90% of traders who blow up their accounts do so because of poor risk management — NOT because of bad trade ideas. Having a great setup means nothing if you risk 20% of your account on one trade.

Position Sizing — The 1–2% Rule

Risk per Trade = Account Size × Risk % (max 1–2%)
Example: $10,000 account × 1% = $100 risk per trade

Position Size = Risk Amount ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Loss Price)
Example: $100 ÷ ($50.00 − $48.50) = $100 ÷ $1.50 = 66 shares

Risk-Reward Ratio (R:R)

Every trade must have a minimum 2:1 risk-reward ratio. That means you target to make at least 2x what you're risking. At 2:1, you only need to be right 34% of the time to break even.

❌

1:1 Risk-Reward

Risk $100 to make $100. You need to be right >50% of the time to be profitable. After commissions and fees — very difficult long-term. Avoid this.

Avoid
✅

2:1 Risk-Reward

Risk $100 to make $200. Win rate needed: 34%+. This is the MINIMUM acceptable for most trading strategies. You can be wrong 2 out of 3 trades and still break even.

Minimum
🏆

3:1 Risk-Reward

Risk $100 to make $300. Win rate needed: only 26%+. Elite setups only. These trades come from patient waiting at key levels with high conviction confluence.

Target

🏗️ Market Structure
Understanding the big picture — timeframes, cycles, and how markets actually move

Market structure is the study of how price organizes itself over time. Before placing any trade, you need to know: What is the bigger trend? Where are we in the cycle? Only then do you zoom into your trading timeframe.

Multi-Timeframe Analysis (MTF)

1

Weekly Chart → Determine the Major Trend

This is your map. Is the overall trend up, down, or sideways? Never trade against the weekly trend unless you are very advanced. If weekly is in an uptrend, only look for long setups on lower timeframes.

2

Daily Chart → Find the Setup

This is your planning timeframe. Look for key support/resistance, pattern formations, and indicator alignment. Most swing traders live here. This is where you identify WHERE to trade.

3

4-Hour / 1-Hour Chart → Time the Entry

Now zoom in. Use this to find the precise entry point and set your stop loss more accurately. Wait for a trigger candle or mini pattern on this timeframe before entering.

4

15-Min / 5-Min → Fine-Tune the Entry (Optional)

Day traders use this level. For swing traders, don't go lower than 1-hour — you'll over-optimize and create noise-driven decisions.

Market Cycles

📥

Smart money quietly buying after a downtrend. Price is sideways, volume is low and building. Most retail traders are still bearish. This is where big moves begin.

Identify Early

Uptrend underway. Early retail traders start joining. Volume increasing. This is the best phase to be long — ride the trend, buy pullbacks to moving averages.

Best to Trade Long
📤

Price at highs, smart money selling into retail euphoria. Price action gets choppy. Often involves fake breakouts above highs that quickly fail. Be cautious here.

Reduce Exposure
📉

Downtrend. Retail traders holding losses and hoping for recovery. Best to be short or cash. Bounces to moving averages are selling opportunities, not recoveries.

Fibonacci — The Most Practical Advanced Tool

Draw Fibonacci from the swing low to swing high (or vice versa). Price tends to find support/resistance at these mathematical levels.

Key Fibonacci Levels to Watch:
23.6% → Minor pullback level (shallow retracement — strong trend)
38.2% → Common in strong trends — often holds as support
50.0% → Not a true Fibonacci but psychologically very important
61.8% → "The Golden Ratio" — most reliable level. Strong bounce point
78.6% → Deep retracement — last chance before a trend reversal

🔑 Best trades: Fibonacci level + horizontal S/R + moving average = Triple confluence

Entry & Exit Strategies

📥 Entry Strategies

  • Wait for candle CLOSE confirmation — not just price touch
  • Enter on retests of breakout levels (reduce risk vs. chasing)
  • Scale in: enter 50% at setup, add 50% on confirmation
  • Pre-plan entries — set limit orders, don't react emotionally
  • Never enter in the last 30 min of a trading session (choppy)

📤 Exit Strategies

  • Scale out: sell 50% at first target, move stop to breakeven on rest
  • Take partial profits at key resistance levels
  • Never move your stop loss wider — only move it to protect profit
  • Exit if thesis is invalidated — price, not time, triggers exit
  • Have a plan before entry — know exactly when/where you're wrong

🧠 Trading Psychology
The final edge — your mindset determines whether all your technical knowledge translates into profit

You can have the best system in the world and still lose money because of emotions. Fear, greed, FOMO, revenge trading — these are the enemies. Professional traders are not smarter than retail traders. They are more disciplined and emotionally consistent.

⚠️ The Brutal Truth After 1 year of trading, if you've been inconsistent, the problem is almost certainly psychology — not your strategy. You likely know what to do but you're not doing it. The gap between knowing and doing is where most traders fail.
😨

Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)

Chasing trades that have already moved significantly. Entering at the worst possible time — top of the move. There's always another trade. Missing a trade is FREE. Taking a bad trade costs you money.

Most Common
🤬

Revenge Trading

Taking random trades to "win back" money after a loss. This is how small losses become catastrophic losses. If you lose 2 trades in a row, STOP for the day. Walk away. Come back tomorrow.

Most Dangerous
💭

Hope Trading

Holding a losing trade because you "hope" it comes back. Hope is not a strategy. If price hit your stop loss level, that's the market telling you the thesis is wrong. Exit.

Account Killer
🎯

Overconfidence

After a winning streak, increasing position size dramatically or taking lower-quality setups. Markets humiliate overconfidence quickly. Keep process consistent regardless of results.

Common Trap

The Professional Trading Mindset Framework

1

Think in Probabilities, Not Certainties

No trade is guaranteed. You are playing a probability game. A loss is not a failure if you followed your rules. A win on a rule-breaking trade is actually bad — it reinforces bad behavior.

2

Separate Process from Outcome

You control your entry, stop loss, position size, and target. You don't control what price does. Judge yourself on whether you followed the plan — not whether you made money on that specific trade.

3

Develop a Pre-Market Routine

Professional traders prepare every morning: review overnight news, scan for setups, mark key levels, and write out their plan. Winging it leads to emotional decisions. A plan keeps you disciplined.

4

Embrace Losses as the Cost of Doing Business

Losses are not mistakes — they are the cost of operating your strategy. If your edge has a 60% win rate, 40% of trades will lose. Accept it. The only real mistake is a loss that wasn't planned for in your risk management.

🗺️ Your Learning Roadmap
6–12 month structured plan to go from 1-year trader to consistently profitable professional

You have 1 year of experience — you have seen real market conditions. Now it's time to build systematically. Don't rush. One month on each phase is a minimum. Stay in the phase until you're consistently applying the skills before moving on.

Phase 1 — Month 1–2

Master Candlesticks & Price Action (Foundation)

Study every major candlestick pattern. Spend 2 hours per day on a naked chart with NO indicators. Identify trends, support/resistance, and candle patterns. Read "Japanese Candlestick Charting" by Steve Nison. This phase is boring for experienced traders but absolutely critical.

Phase 2 — Month 2–3

Chart Patterns + Volume Analysis

Learn the top 10 chart patterns with volume confirmation rules. Practice identifying them on historical charts. Start a trade journal NOW even if you're not trading yet — journal your pattern observations. Read "How to Make Money in Stocks" by William O'Neil for pattern mastery.

Phase 3 — Month 3–5

Indicators + Risk Management System

Add moving averages, RSI, and MACD to your charts. But only use them to CONFIRM what price action is showing. Simultaneously build your risk management rules: position sizing formula, max daily loss, max drawdown rules. These rules must be written down. Test live with small size.

Phase 4 — Month 5–8

Build & Backtest Your Trading Strategy

Combine everything into ONE clear strategy with specific rules. Backtest it on at least 100 historical trades. What's the win rate? Average R:R? Max drawdown? A strategy without verified historical edge is gambling. Only advance to live trading with larger size when you have 100+ backtested trades AND 30+ live trades showing consistency.

Phase 5 — Month 8–12

Advanced Tools + Psychology Mastery

Add Fibonacci, multi-timeframe analysis, and one advanced concept (Wyckoff or Elliott Wave). Focus heavily on trading psychology. Read "Trading in the Zone" by Mark Douglas — it will change everything. Review your journal weekly. Identify your psychological patterns and build protocols to manage them.
